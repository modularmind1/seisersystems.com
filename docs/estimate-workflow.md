# Estimate Workflow

This document describes how Seiser Systems estimates should work in a future private operations system.

## Estimate Lifecycle

1. Estimate starts as draft.
2. Customer request and consultation notes are reviewed.
3. Recommended work, labor, parts, and notes are drafted.
4. Tanner reviews the estimate before sending.
5. Estimate is sent to the customer.
6. Customer approves, declines, or requests changes.
7. Approved estimates become the basis for scheduling and job tracking.

## Required Estimate Fields

- Customer info
- Problem / goal
- Recommended work
- Labor estimate
- Parts estimate
- Total estimate
- Notes
- Approval statement

## Review Rules

- Estimates must be reviewed by Tanner before sending.
- Estimates should clearly state that pricing may change if scope changes.
- Estimates should avoid unclear promises, hidden assumptions, or unnecessary upselling.
- Estimates should be stored only in a future private operations system or approved private workspace.

## Scope Change Language

Every estimate should communicate that pricing is based on the information available at the time of evaluation and may change if:

- The issue is different than initially described.
- Additional problems are discovered.
- Parts are unavailable or change in price.
- The customer requests additional work.
